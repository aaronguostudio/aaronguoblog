Many ideas once stayed on paper not because they were weak, but because execution was expensive. Building a team, making a product, and testing a market could drain the momentum from a thought before it had a chance to become real.

AI is lowering that barrier. One person can send several agents down different paths, turn a rough idea into small experiments, and use real feedback to decide what deserves another round.

What grows scarce may no longer be execution alone, or the vague ability to “have ideas.” It is closing the loop: notice a problem, design an experiment, judge the result, and write the learning back into the system. For people and organizations, the new question is the same: can you make the next attempt better?
